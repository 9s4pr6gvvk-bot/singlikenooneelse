Music Video Direction

Bring your music to life visually. Concept, production and direction — made by someone who does it for their own music.

The Work

Your music deserves
to be seen.

I make all my own music videos. Every single one — concept, production, direction, editing. Not because I had a budget for a director, but because the visual side of what I do matters to me and I wanted control over it.

That experience is what I bring to this. I understand what it is to work with limited resources, to shoot on a small crew, to make something that looks intentional on a budget that isn't. Constraints can be creative. Some of the most striking videos are the ones that found something specific to say rather than something expensive to show.

We work together on concept, treatment, production planning and direction. I can work with whatever you have — a proper shoot budget, a phone, or anything in between.

Identity
Is This For You?

This work suits artists who…

Are releasing music and want a video that actually represents them — not a generic performance clip.

Have a concept but don't know how to execute it — or aren't sure if the concept is right.

Are working with a limited budget and want to make something good anyway.

Want their videos to feel like part of a coherent visual identity across releases.

Have never made a video before and want guidance through the whole process from concept to completion.

Want to learn to direct their own videos rather than being dependent on hiring someone every time.

The Approach

How I direct

I have directed every music video I have ever released — entirely independently. What I know about making videos I learned by doing it with no crew, no budget and no formal training. That practical knowledge is exactly what I offer here.

01

The music leads

Every visual decision should serve the song. We never impose an aesthetic that doesn't belong to the music.

02

Constraints are creative

A limited budget forces specificity. Often the most memorable videos are the ones that found one strong idea and committed to it.

03

Your identity, not mine

The goal is a video that looks and feels unmistakably like you. I'm here to realise your vision, not impose my own.

04

Plan properly

Most problems on a video shoot happen because of insufficient planning. We work out as much as possible before you pick up a camera.

05

Learn as you go

I want you to understand what we're doing and why — so that your next video is easier and stronger than your last.

06

Done from scratch

I have directed every video I have released entirely independently. Everything I offer comes from doing it, not studying it.
